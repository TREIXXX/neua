About

NEU Repository is a digital repository dedicated to preserving and showcasing the exceptional academic work produced by students of New Era University.

Our mission is to:
  • Provide a centralized platform for accessing student research and projects
  • Promote academic excellence and knowledge sharing
  • Preserve the intellectual output of the university community
  • Serve as a resource for current and future students, faculty, and researchers

Currently, NEU Repository focuses on scholarly works from the Department of Computer Studies, including programs in Information Technology, Computer Science, Information Systems, and Library and Information Science.

Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 18, 2025

Information Collection

NEU Repository collects limited personal information when users access the platform. For authors, we store basic metadata including name, degree program, and year of publication along with your scholarly work.

Use of Information

The information collected is used solely for academic purposes within the New Era University community. Your work may be accessed by university members and the public for research and reference purposes.

Copyright

Authors retain copyright of their scholarly works. By submitting to NEU Repository, you grant the university a non-exclusive license to preserve and provide access to your work.

Data Security

We implement appropriate security measures to protect against unauthorized access or disclosure of your information.

Changes to This Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy periodically. Any changes will be posted on this page.

Frequently Asked Questions

NEU Repository is a digital platform designed to preserve and showcase the exceptional academic work produced by New Era University students particulartly in the CICS Department.

Think of it as a virtual library where you can find a wide range of capstone projects, from groundbreaking research to creative innovations. NEU Repository serves as a valuable resource for students, faculty, and the public to explore and appreciate the intellectual achievements of the university's community.

Currently, NEU Repository primarily focuses on scholarly works from the Department of Computer Studies. This includes theses and dissertations from programs like BSIT (Bachelor of Science in Information Technology), BSCS (Bachelor of Science in Computer Science), BSIS (Bachelor of Science in Information Systems), and BLIS (Library and Information Science). While NEU Repository may expand its coverage to include other departments in the future, its current focus is on these computer-related fields.

Only authorized administrators can upload scholarly works to NEU Repository. This ensures that the content uploaded to the platform meets the university's quality standards and is approved by faculty members.

If you have a scholarly work you'd like to be considered for inclusion in NEU Repository, you should submit it to your professor for review and potential approval. Once your work is approved, the professor or another authorized administrator will be responsible for uploading it to the NEU Repository platform.

The authors own the copyrights to their scholarly works. This means you have the exclusive right to control how your work is used, reproduced, distributed, or modified.

However, it's important to be aware of any copyright agreements or policies that may be in place at your university. These policies might outline certain rights or restrictions related to the use of your thesis or dissertation, especially if the university has a digital repository where it's stored.

If you have any specific questions about copyright ownership and your thesis or dissertation, it's advisable to consult with your university's library or legal counsel.

Once a scholarly work is uploaded, it cannot be replaced or modified by the user. If updates are necessary, please contact the NEU Repository support team to request a review.

To cite a thesis from NEU Repository, please follow the citation format used by your department or course. Typically, citations should include the author's name, thesis title, publication year, and a link to the repository entry.

Removal of a scholarly work from NEU Repository is possible but requires a formal request. Please contact the NEU Repository support team with your reasons, and the request will be reviewed by the administration.

Only authorized administrators can upload scholarly works to NEU Repository. This ensures that the content uploaded to the platform meets the university's quality standards and is approved by faculty members.

If you encounter any issues while using NEU Repository, the best way to report them is to contact your department or advisor. They can provide specific guidance on how to proceed and help you resolve the problem.

You can usually reach out to your department or advisor via email or by scheduling an appointment. They will be able to assist you in navigating the NEU Repository platform and addressing any technical difficulties or concerns you may have.
