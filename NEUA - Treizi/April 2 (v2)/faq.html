Frequently Asked Questions

NEUA stands for New Era University Archives. It's a digital platform designed to preserve and showcase the exceptional academic work produced by New Era University students.

Think of it as a virtual library where you can find a wide range of capstone projects, from groundbreaking research to creative innovations. NEUA serves as a valuable resource for students, faculty, and the public to explore and appreciate the intellectual achievements of the university's community.

Currently, NEUA primarily focuses on scholarly works from the Department of Computer Studies. This includes theses and dissertations from programs like BSIT (Bachelor of Science in Information Technology), BSCS (Bachelor of Science in Computer Science), and BSIS (Bachelor of Science in Information Systems). While NEUA may expand its coverage to include other departments in the future, its current focus is on these computer-related fields.

Only authorized administrators can upload scholarly works to NEUA. This ensures that the content uploaded to the platform meets the university's quality standards and is approved by faculty members.

If you have a scholarly work you'd like to be considered for inclusion in NEUA, you should submit it to your professor for review and potential approval. Once your work is approved, the professor or another authorized administrator will be responsible for uploading it to the NEUA platform.

The authors own the copyrights to their scholarly works. This means you have the exclusive right to control how your work is used, reproduced, distributed, or modified.

However, it's important to be aware of any copyright agreements or policies that may be in place at your university. These policies might outline certain rights or restrictions related to the use of your thesis or dissertation, especially if the university has a digital repository where it's stored.

If you have any specific questions about copyright ownership and your thesis or dissertation, it's advisable to consult with your university's library or legal counsel.

Once a scholarly work is uploaded, it cannot be replaced or modified by the user. If updates are necessary, please contact the NEUA support team to request a review.

To cite a thesis from NEUA, please follow the citation format used by your department or course. Typically, citations should include the author's name, thesis title, publication year, and a link to the repository entry.

Removal of a scholarly work from NEUA is possible but requires a formal request. Please contact the NEUA support team with your reasons, and the request will be reviewed by the administration.

Only authorized administrators can upload scholarly works to NEUA. This ensures that the content uploaded to the platform meets the university's quality standards and is approved by faculty members.

If you encounter any issues while using NEUA, the best way to report them is to contact your department or advisor. They can provide specific guidance on how to proceed and help you resolve the problem.

You can usually reach out to your department or advisor via email or by scheduling an appointment. They will be able to assist you in navigating the NEUA platform and addressing any technical difficulties or concerns you may have.
